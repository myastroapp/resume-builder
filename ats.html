How it works

Most companies run resumes through an applicant tracking system (ATS) that screens for the skills and terms in the job posting before a human ever reads it. This tool pulls the most frequent meaningful keywords from the job description, then checks which appear in your resume. Add the missing ones only where they're genuinely true of you, using the exact wording from the posting — that's what raises your match.

Tips

  • Mirror the posting's exact terms ("JavaScript" not "JS") — ATS keyword matching is literal.
  • Put key skills in your summary and a skills section, not only buried in bullets.
  • Never keyword-stuff or claim skills you don't have — a human reads it next.
