Resume summary examples

A resume summary is the 2–3 line pitch at the top of your resume. Done well, it tells a recruiter in five seconds why you're a fit. Below is a simple formula and eight examples you can adapt.

Build your resume free →

The formula

[Role + years] + [your strongest skills] + [a result or specialty]. Lead with what you are, follow with proof. Tailor the last part to the job you're applying for.

8 examples

Software engineer
Full-stack engineer with 6 years building web apps in React and Node. Shipped features used by 1M+ users and cut page-load time 40%. Looking to bring product-minded engineering to a fast-growing team.
Entry-level / new grad
Recent computer-science graduate with internship experience in Python and data analysis. Built a class-project app adopted by 200 students. Eager to apply strong fundamentals and a fast learning curve to a junior developer role.
Career changer
Marketing professional transitioning into UX design, with a completed design certificate and three end-to-end portfolio projects. Combines five years of understanding customers with new design skills to build interfaces people actually use.
Project / program manager
PMP-certified project manager with 8 years leading cross-functional teams. Delivered 30+ projects on time and 12% under budget. Skilled at turning ambiguous goals into clear plans stakeholders trust.
Sales
B2B account executive with a 5-year record of exceeding quota (142% of target in 2025). Strong at consultative selling and growing existing accounts. Seeking a senior closing role with room to mentor.
Registered nurse
Compassionate RN with 4 years in acute-care settings and BLS/ACLS certification. Known for calm patient communication and a spotless medication-safety record. Looking to grow in a busy hospital unit.
Customer support
Customer-support specialist with 3 years resolving 50+ tickets a day at a 97% satisfaction rate. Skilled in Zendesk and turning frustrated customers into loyal ones. Ready for a team-lead step up.
Recent career return
Operations professional returning to work after a planned career break, with 7 years of prior experience streamlining logistics for a mid-size retailer. Quick to re-engage and eager to apply proven process-improvement skills.

Quick tips

  • Write it last — after your experience, you'll know your strongest points.
  • Use numbers. "Cut costs 15%" beats "reduced costs."
  • Skip "hard-working team player." Show it instead of saying it.
  • Keep it to 2–3 lines and tailor it to each job.
